Sarmi

About:

The Stuffed Cabbage Rolls are a very tasty dish and a big part of the Bulgarian cuisine. They can be prepared with or without meat and can be served hot or cold.

Ingredients

  • 10 large cabbage leaves or 20 small pickled
  • 1 large onion finely chopped
  • 3,5 oz/ 100 g cremini mushrooms finely chopped
  • 1 large carrot finely chopped
  • 1 ⅓ cup/ 230 g rice any kind
  • ⅓ cup/ 50 g raisins optional
  • 2 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried savory
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley finely chopped, optional
  • ½ teaspoon dried spearmint optional
  • salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Cut the large leaves in two, removing the hard ribs at the bottom of each leaf. Stack the leaves on a plate and set aside.
  2. Heat the oil in a non stick pan and sauté the onion for 1-2 minutes. Add the carrots and the mushrooms and cook for another 5 minutes, stirring from time to time.
  3. Add the rice and cook for few minutes, until translucent. Add the spices and the raisins, stir and remove from heat.
  4. Extend the cabbage leaves on a kitchen board. Fill each one with 1-2 tablespoon of the filling, depending on the size of the leaf. Wrap the filling into the leaf and roll it.
  5. Arrange the cabbage rolls in a pot with the wrapped part facing the bottom. Place them closely one next to another. You can make 2 or 3 layers of sarmi, depending on how big is your pot.
  6. Place an inverted plate on top of your sarmi and cover the pot with a lid. Pour 1 – 1,5 cups of water into the pot: make sure the water level reaches the top roll sarmi so that the rice gets cooked.
  7. Cook for 25-30 minutes on the stove at a low heat. Don’t allow the water to boil violently so that the cabbage rolls stay neat.
  8. The dish is ready when the rice is completely cooked. Remove from heat and serve immediately with some of the sauce from the pot.
